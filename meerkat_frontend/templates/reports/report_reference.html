Jordan · Public Health Surveillance

Public Health Profile Week 24 · 17 June 2015

Key findings
ï¿¼

Information reported in this public health profile represents cumulative cases reported from 234 outpatient clinics in Jordan up until end of Epidemiological Week 24 (17 June 2015).

The system began reporting in a phased manner from 6 April 2015.

  • 2,343 cases reported from 234 reporting sites across the country
  • 45.0% female and 34.1% under five years of age
  • 75.8% morbidity due to communicable disease, including acute diarrhoea, chicken pox, bloody diarrhoea and meningitis
  • 23.1% morbidity due to non-communicable disease, including diabetes mellitus, hypertension and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease

Alerts

  • 36 alerts of suspected bloody diarrhoea
  • 17 alerts of suspected measles
  • 9 alerts of suspected mumps
  • 7 alerts of suspected brucellosis
  • 5 alerts of suspected leishmaniasis

More Information

ï¿¼A program of primary care and public health surveillance is being implemented in 234 outpatient clinics in Jordan (every MoH hospital, prison, mental health facility and 80% of primary and comprehensive centres), in partnership with WHO and Ministry of Health. The project introduces case-based, integrated disease surveillance of mental health, non-communicable disease and communicable disease.

The clinician uses the system within the consultation, which introduces clinical- decision support for child health and mental health, as well as best practice prescribing guidance and and real-time reporting of information. Information is made available within one hour via an online framework with automated generation of SMS and email alerts and support for mapping and reporting.
